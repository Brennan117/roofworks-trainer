3 · Surface candidates

Scans each photo's raw pixels for hail-sized dark tonal spots that stand out from the surrounding shingle field — shadow lines, streaks and big patches are filtered by shape. Free and instant. Candidates rank by contrast strength (σ); if AI-classify is on, Claude then grades each found spot and the rank blends both.

Gold = your clearest, textbook examples — those become the model's reference images. Grade fast; “Unsure” is fine and is kept out of the reference set.

4 · Update the model from your grades

Two things happen: (a) your Gold + Hail crops become positive reference examples and your high-scoring Not crops become counter-examples; (b) your grades calibrate the raw score into a real probability + a suggested threshold. Then surface a fresh set and it should rank better.
